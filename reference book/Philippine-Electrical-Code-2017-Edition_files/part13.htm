ARTICLE 9.13 - MOTORS, MOTOR CIRCUITS, AND CONTROLLERS


9.13.1 General


9.13.1.1 Scope. Motors, motor circuits, and controllers shall comply with the requirements of this article, in addition to the applicable provisions of Article 4.30.


9.13.1.2 Installation Requirements.

(A) In Dangerous and Hazardous Spaces or Zones. Motors, either for permanent installation or portable mounted for positioning in dangerous and hazardous spaces or zones, and for use in various services and specific undertakings of the watercraft, as well as propulsion motors, are generally of special construction and materials designed for marine application.

(B) Similar Requirements for Generators. The construction and materials, insulation, excitation, lubrication and testing of motors shall be in accordance with similar requirements for generators in Section

9.10.1.4 and Part 9.10.2, except that Sections 9.10.1.6, 9.10.2.6, 9.10.2.?(A) and (B) are applicable only for propulsion motors.

(C) Motors Not Mounted with theRotor Shafts. Wben motors for service at sea are not mounted with the rotor shafts in a fore-and-aft direction, the type of bearing and lubrication shaJl require special consideration.

(D) Location. Wben intrinsically-safe components are located by necessity within enclosures that contain non­ intrinsically-safe systems, such as control consoles and motor starters, such components shall be effectively isolated in a sub-compartment by physical barriers having a suitable cover or panel secured by bolts, locks, allen-screws, or other approved methods.

(E) Inside Refrigerated Cargo Compartment. Motors

in the air stream inside refrigerated cargo compartment shall be totally enclosed or otherwise suitably enclosed to withstand the effects of excessive moisture where they are fitted between the two sections of a cooler or where the air from the cooler passes over the motor.


9.13.1.3 Installation Approval. Where permitted, motors of the explosion proof type and intrinsically - safe electrical instruments, circuitry and devices shall be of an approved type.


9.13.2 Nameplates


9.13.2.1 All Motors. All motors shall be fitted with nameplates of corrosion-resistant material marked in accordance with Section 4.30.1.7.


9.13.3 Motor Temperature Ratings


9.13.3.1 Maximum Allowable Temperature. The maximum allowable temperature for propulsion motors and those for engine and fireroom auxiliaries shall be in accordance with Table 9.13.3.l for de and ac motors, based on an ambient temperature of 50°C. (See Tables 9.18.2.1-1 and 9.18.2.1-2).


9.13.3.2 Ambient Temperature of 40°C. Where provision is made for insuring an ambient temperature being maintained at 40°C or less, as by air cooling or by location outside of the boiler and engine rooms, the allowable temperature of the winding hall be l0°C higher.


9.13.3.3 Deck-winch and Direct-acting Capstan Motors. ·Deck-winch and direct-acting capstan motors shall be rated on a full-load run at least 0.5 hour.


9.13.3.4 Direct-acting Windlass Motors. Direct­ acting windlass motors shall be rated on a full-load run of at least 0.25 hour.


9.13.3.5 Hydraulic Transmission Operation. Those operating through hydraulic transmission shall be rated for 30 minutes idle pump operation.


9.13.4 Watercraft's Service Motors


9.13.4.1 General. Motors for use in the engine room or spaces where subject to mechanical injury, or dripping of oil or vapor or water, shall be of the open type if protected by drip covers, otherwise they shall be waterproof or dripproof. Care shall be exercised in locating the open type and dripproof type high enough

to avoid bilge water. Motors below the level of floor plates shall be waterproof or have all openings for ventilation located above the floor-plate level.


9.13.4.2 Pump Motors. Motors for operating plunger and close-coupled pumps shall have the driving end entirely enclosed or designed to prevent leakage from entering the motor.


9.13.4.3 Motors on Weather Decks. Motors for use on weather decks shall be of the watertight type or shall be enclosed in watertight housings.


9.13.4.4 Motors Below Decks. Motors installed below decks shall be located in as dry a place as practicable and proximity to steam, water and oil pipings shall be avoided.


9.13.4.5 Motors for Bulk-Carriers. For use of motors on vessels of the bulk-oil-carrier type and vessels carrying oil having a flash point less than 80°C only explosion-proof types of construction shall be used. (See Article 9.19)

ARTICLE 9.14 -PERMANENT WATERTIGHT FIXTURES, PORTABLE EQUIPMENT, PLUGS, RECEPTACLES, AND OTHER SIMILAR APPARATUS


9.14.1 General


9.14.1.1 Permanent Watertight Fixtures.

(A) Corrosion Resistant. Pennanent watertight :fixtures shall be corrosion-resistant and shall be used where exposed to the weather or splashing water. Lighting fixtures of this type shall be rendered watertight by means of glass globes protected by substantial guards.

(B) Exception. Watertight lighting fixtures shall not be required for any interior locations, except refrigerated compartments, chain lockers when permanently illuminated or where exposed to splashing water.

9.14.1.2 Receptacles and Plugs of Different Electrical Ratings. Receptacles and plugs of different electrical ratings shall not be interchangeable. In cases where it is necessary to use 230 volts portable equipment, the receptacles for their attachment shall be of a type, which will not pennit attaching 115 volts equipment.


9.14.1.3 Portable Equipment and Outlets. Portable apparatus is any apparatus served by a flexible cord. Portable apparatus shall not be used in cargo oil pump rooms or other hazardous locations nor are portable lights shall be used for berth lights in passenger accommodations or crew's quarters.

{A) Watertight Outlets. Watertight outlets shall be used where exposed to the weather or in spaces subject to severe moisture conditions.

(B) Grounding. Receptacle outlets operating at 50 volts or more shall have a grounding pole. Attachment plugs for pennanently fitted equipment operating at 50 volts or more shall have a grounding pole and a grounding conductor in the portable cord to ground the noncurrent carrying metal parts of the equipment.

(C) Splices, Splices shall not be made in portable cords for permanently fitted equipment.

ARTICLE 9.16 - SEMICONDUCTOR RECTIFIER FOR POWER


9.16.1 General


9.16.1.1 Application. The requirements in this article shall apply to the semiconductor rectifiers including thyristor for power rating not less than 5 kW.


9.16.2 Construction and Location


9.16.2.1 Construction.

(A) Removable. Rectifier units, rectifier stacks or cells shall be so arranged that they can be removed from equipment without dismantling the complete unit.

(B) Suitably Installed or Protected. Air-cooled rectifiers shall be suitably installed or protected against the effects of salty air and humidity.

9.16.2.2 Location.

9.16.3.1 Protective Devices.

(A) Forced Cooling. Where forced cooling is provided, the rectifier shall be so arranged that the rectifier cannot remain loaded unless effective cooling is maintained.

(B) Protection Against Transient Overvoltage. Means shall be provided to guard against transient overvoltage caused by switching and breaking of the circuits and de voltage rise due to regenerative power.

(C) Fuses for Rectifier Cells. Protecting fuses for rectifier cells shall be coordinated with the characteristic of the rectifier cell.

9.16.3.3 Transformer for Rectifiers. Transformer for rectifiers shall be of two separate windings.

9.16.4.1 Gate Control Circuits. Gate control circuits

shall comply with the following requirements:

(A) Arrangement. Gate control circuits of thyristors shall be so arranged that they can generate the gate pulse not exceeding the gate rating and have the pulse wide enough to fire all thyristors connected. The gate control circuits shall also be protected from misfire caused by electrostatic induction and or electro-magnetic induction.

(B) Series or Parallel Connection. Where thyristors arc connected in series or parallel, gate control circuits shall be so arranged that firing timings of each thyristor js re!,TUlar.


9.16.4.2 Thyristor Control for DC Motor. Where de motors are controlled by thyristor, the following requirements shall be applied:

(A) Harmonic Effect. Where commutation of de motor may be affected by the harmonics of thyristor output waveform, appropriate measures shall be taken to reduce such harmonics.

(B) Lower Power Factor Effect. Where electric sources may be affected by lower power factor resulting from the phase control of thyristor, means shall be provided to compensate it.

(C) Changing-over the Field Polarity. In case where motors are operated in either direction of rotation by means of changing-over the field polarity, interlock shall be made so as to reverse the polarity of field after armature-current reaches zero, and in addition, suitable means shall be provided to limit electrical nonlocked conditions of armature.


9.16.5 Semiconductor Rectifiers for Propulsion


9.16.5.1 General.

(A) Corrosion-Resistant. The enclosures of semiconductor rectifiers for propulsion and other parts subject to corrosion shall be made of corrosion-resistant material or of a material rendered corrosion-resistant.

(B) Performance Criteria. The temperature rise of semiconductor rectifiers for propulsion under all conditions sball be limited to such a value as will pennit the rectifier to meet the specified perfonnance criteria.

Rectifiers shall be installed away from sources of radiant energy in locations where the circulation of air is not restricted to and from the rectifier and where the temperature of the inlet air to air-cooled rectifiers will not exceed that for which the rectifier is designed.

(B) Nonflanimable Liquid. Immersed-type rectifiers shall use a nonflammable liquid.

ARTICLE 9.17 - CONTROLGEARS


9.17.1 Construction


9.17.1.1 General.

(A) Durable Construction. Controlgears shall be durably constructed and provided with efficient means of starting, stopping, reversing and speed control of motor� together with essential safety devices.

(B) Protective Enclosure. Controlgears shall have suitable protective enclosures depending on their location and shall be so constructed that operators can handle them safely.

(q Intrinsically Safe Electrical Devices. In case where intrinsically safe electrical devices are built in controlgears, they shall be arranged for independent installation from other electrical equipment, that is, the wires of intrinsically-safe circuits shall be separated from other circuits, and be shielded electrically, if necessary. Suitable measures shall be taken to identify the wires for intrinsically-safe circuits easily.

9.17.1.2 Wearing Parts. All wearing parts of controlgears shall be readily renewable and accessible for inspection and maintenance.

9.17.1.3 Controlgears for Mot�rs Larger than 0.5 kW. Motors larger than 0.5 kW shall be provided with the following control apparatus:

(A) Means to Prevent Undesired Restarting. Means shall be provided to prevent undesired restarting after stoppage due to low voltage or complete loss of voltage. This requirement does not apply to motors, the continuous availability of which is essential to the safety of the watercraft and the automatic operation.

(B) Isolating Means. Means of isolation shall be provided so that all voltages may be cut off from the motor, except where means of isolation (that provided at the switchboard, section board, distribution board, etc.) are adjacent to the motor.

(C) Automatic Disconnection. Means of automatic disconnection of the supply shall be provided in the event of excess current due to mechanical overloading of the motor. This requirement shall not apply to the motors for steering gear power units (steering motors).
